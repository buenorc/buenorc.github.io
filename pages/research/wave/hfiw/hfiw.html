High-frequency internal waves

Based on vertical velocities recorded every 1 minute we observed the propagation of high-frequency internal waves in a Passauna reservoir (Fig. 1), including high-frequency internal waves with higher vertical mode (Fig. 2).

Now we are investigating the source of these high-frequency internal waves (HFIWs) and how they may influence the dissipation of BSIW in the lakeshore. We use meteorological data, underwater temperature, and low-frequency sensors measuring the horizontal velocity in two components (5 minutes) to investigate deeply the dynamic of high-frequency internal waves field. To analyze if large-scale motion is associated with those HFIW, we are also using numerical models to understand the dynamic of large-scale flows in Passauna reservoirs, where we did not identify a dominant internal seiche activity.

We observed that the high-frequency internal waves have been observed during periods of high horizontal shear velocity which is directly associated with the wind field that acts at the lake surface.

Thus, we suggest that the high-frequency waves observed were excited due to the shear velocity of the first superficial layer, which transfers kinetic energy to the region of the thermocline.

However, more investigation needs to be conducted to highlight the large-scale flow (which some preliminary results suggest is not due to internal seiche activity).

Some observations have suggested that the interfacial shear between layers just provides an increase of HFIW energy, but even in the absence of interfacial shear, we may observe low-amplitude HFIWs.

In addition, during some periods we also have detected the propagation of HFIW of the second vertical mode (Fig 2).
