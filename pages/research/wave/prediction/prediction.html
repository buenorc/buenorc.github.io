Internal seiche amplitude

We have analyzed the internal seiche amplitude of BSIW from temperature measurements for field measurements (from Vossoroca reservoir and Harp Lake) and compared with theoretical amplitude, estimated by theoretical results [4].

We have detected BSIW in Vossoroca Reservoir and Harp Lake with different baroclinic modes (For more information click here). We have observed that most of the wave is formed during the winter season in Vossoroca Reservoir and in Autumn in Harp Lake, suggesting that higher amplitude internal seiches are excited during periods of low stability [4], which corroborates with previous theoretical results (Fig. 1).

The system stability can be described by the Wedderburn number (W)

\[W=\frac{g'~h_e^2}{L~u^2}\]

in which g' is the reduced gravity, he is the upper mixed layer, L is the basin length, and u* is the velocity friction of the wind.

However, although the trend is well described by previous theory, the estimation fails to predict the internal seiche amplitude when the stratification deviates from the idealized two-layer system (Fig 1).

We observed a much smaller amplitude for a really small W. To understand why the internal seiche was limited in some thermal conditions we ran 32 simulations to analyze the lake response to a wind event (varying W from 10 to 0.1).

Numerical observations have suggested that the amplitude of internal seiches is not just correlated to the W, but is affected by other variables, such as the ratio of the depth of the thermocline and the total water depth (he/H), the resonance effect with the wind, and the turbulence production near the wave crest [6]. We observed that the measured results (which include also observation of the wide literature) are not well described by previously established theories.

The wave amplitude is directly associated with the daily Wedderburn number of the analyzed period at the thermocline depth for fundamental internal seiche mode [4]. The overall result, more clear for V1H1 internal seiche mode observed in Vossoroca reservoir, shows an inversely proportional relationship between internal wave amplitude and the Wedderburn number, with higher variation for different he/H.

We observed that for a low Wedderburn number (W<"1"), the instabilities due to boundary interactions reduce the basin-scale internal wave amplitude, and the increase of the wave does not follow the previous mathematical formulation anymore [5,6]. This observation was also evidenced through three-dimensional numerical simulations [6].

For a shallow upper layer, the three-dimensional model also indicated that the increase of pressure at the upper layer, leads to a higher internal seiche compared with previous theories (Fig. 2). Based on numerical data, and supported by field observations, we proposed a new parameterization to better describe the excitation of basin-scale internal waves in thermal-stratified basins [6]:

\[\frac{\zeta_o}{h_e} = A\exp\Bigg(\frac{-(W-k)^2}{2~f^2}\Bigg)\]

where W is the Wedderburn number, k = 6 and A = 0.1 are universal constants, and f is a function of he/H, which determines an higher order influence of he/H:

\[f(h_e/H) = g(h_e/H)\exp\Bigg(\frac{(h_e/H)^2}{B}\Bigg)\]

where B = 0.125 is a universal constant (found empirically) and the function g is a function of he/H that was found empirically from the numerical simulations and is defined as:

\[g(h_e/H) = 12.156\Bigg(\frac{h_e}{H}\Bigg)^3- 15.714\Bigg(\frac{h_e}{H}\Bigg)^2 + 2.843 \frac{h_e}{H} + 2.085\]
